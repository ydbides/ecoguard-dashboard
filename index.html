About EcoGuard

Everything you need to know — explained simply.

🐠 What is EcoGuard?

EcoGuard is like a doctor for your fish tank. It watches your water 24 hours a day, 7 days a week — checking if the water is too hot, too dirty, too acidic, or running low. If something goes wrong, it tells you immediately on this website so you can fix it before your fish get hurt.

🌡
Temperature
Is the water the right warmth for your fish?
💧
Water Level
Is there enough water in the tank?
🌊
Turbidity
Is the water clean or dirty and cloudy?
🧪
pH Level
Is the water too acidic or too alkaline?

⚙️ How Does It Work?

EcoGuard uses a Raspberry Pi 5 — a tiny computer about the size of a credit card — connected to sensors inside your fish tank. Every 3 seconds the Pi reads all the sensors and sends the numbers to the internet (using Google Firebase). This website then shows you those numbers in real time, from anywhere in the world.

🔬 Sensors in the tank → measure temperature, water, turbidity, pH
🍓 Raspberry Pi 5 → reads sensors every 3 seconds
☁️ Firebase (Google Cloud) → stores the data online instantly
🌐 This website → reads Firebase and shows you everything live
🤖 OpenAI (ChatGPT) → the Pi asks AI to analyse the data every 60 seconds and writes a health report

💯 What is the Health Score?

The big circle on the home page shows a Health Score from 0% to 100%. Think of it like a school report for your tank. 100% means everything is perfect. Below 60% means something needs your attention. The score is calculated from all four sensors combined — temperature, water level, turbidity, and pH.

80–100%
Excellent — everything is great!
50–79%
Warning — check something soon
0–49%
Critical — act immediately!

🧪 What is pH and Why Does it Matter?

pH is a number from 0 (very acidic, like battery acid) to 14 (very alkaline, like bleach). Pure water is 7 — perfectly in the middle. Most freshwater fish need the water to be between 6.5 and 7.5. If it goes outside that range, it can burn their gills and kill them — even if the water looks perfectly clean. EcoGuard watches pH constantly and warns you the moment it moves out of the safe zone.

🌊 What is Turbidity?

Turbidity measures how cloudy or dirty the water is. Clean water has low turbidity. When fish produce waste, food breaks down, or algae grows, the water becomes murky and turbidity rises. High turbidity means less oxygen, more bacteria, and stressed fish. EcoGuard automatically turns on the pump when turbidity gets too high to filter the water back to clean.

🔧 What Does EcoGuard Control?

EcoGuard doesn't just watch — it can also take action automatically when something goes wrong. In AUTO mode, the Raspberry Pi controls three devices through relays (electronic switches):

🔥
Heater
Turns ON when water is too cold. Turns OFF when the right temperature is reached.
⚙️
Pump / Filter
Turns ON when the water is too cloudy (high turbidity) to circulate and clean it.
💡
Light
Controlled by the system — stays ON while conditions are safe.

🤖 How Does the AI Work?

Every 60 seconds, the Raspberry Pi takes all the sensor readings and sends them to OpenAI (the same company behind ChatGPT). It asks: "Here is my tank data — is everything OK? What should the owner do?" OpenAI writes back a plain-English answer, which is saved to Firebase and then shown on this website under the health score. The AI knows about your specific fish species and compares the water conditions to what those fish actually need.

🐠 Multi-Fish Stocking Analysis

In the Fish Preferences page you can add every species you keep in your tank. EcoGuard then checks whether all those fish can live together happily — comparing their temperature needs, pH preferences, aggression levels, and minimum tank sizes. This is called stocking analysis and it helps you avoid putting fish together that will fight or need very different water conditions.

🏆 Project Info

EcoGuard was built for the Eskom Expo for Young Scientists. It is a real working system — not a simulation. The Raspberry Pi 5 is physically connected to sensors in a live fish tank, and all the data you see on this dashboard is coming directly from those sensors in real time. The goal is to make fish keeping smarter, safer, and more reliable for everyone.
